Meet James Mitchell

A fifth-generation Alabamian with deep roots in our communities, our values, and our way of life.

Fifth-Generation Alabamian

A Leader Forged in Alabama Values

James Mitchell grew up on a family farm in Cullman County, where he learned the values of hard work, faith, and community that guide him to this day. After serving in the U.S. Army and earning his law degree from the University of Alabama, James returned home to build a life dedicated to serving others.

As a three-term state senator, James has fought to lower taxes, defend Second Amendment rights, protect Alabama's farming communities, and bring high-speed internet to every corner of the state. Now, he's ready to take that fight to the Governor's mansion.

The Mitchell Platform

Commonsense conservative solutions for the challenges facing Alabama's families and communities.

💰

Economic Growth & Jobs

Cut red tape, lower business taxes, and attract major employers to Alabama. Our plan will create 50,000 new jobs and make the Yellowhammer State the best place in America to start a business.

🌾

Protecting Agriculture

Alabama's farmers feed our families and fuel our economy. We'll fight for fair trade deals, expand crop insurance, and invest in agricultural innovation to keep Alabama farms thriving for generations.

📚

Education & Workforce

Every Alabama child deserves a world-class education. We'll expand school choice, raise teacher pay, invest in vocational training, and partner with industry to prepare students for tomorrow's careers.

💪

2nd Amendment Rights

The right to bear arms is non-negotiable. James Mitchell has an A+ rating from the NRA and will veto any legislation that infringes on law-abiding Alabamians' constitutional rights.

⚔️

Faith & Family Values

Our families are the backbone of Alabama. We'll defend the sanctity of life, support faith-based organizations, and ensure parents have the final say in their children's education and upbringing.

💡

Rural Infrastructure

It's time every Alabamian has access to broadband internet, quality roads, and reliable utilities. Our $2 billion infrastructure plan will bridge the urban-rural divide and connect all 67 counties.
