Our Story

Named for a grandmother. Built on fire, memory, and the belief that food brings people together.

The Beginning

A Grandmother’s Legacy

Helen is named for Chef Rob McDaniel’s grandmother — a woman who understood that the most powerful ingredient in any meal is the love with which it’s prepared. Growing up watching her cook over hardwood coals, Rob learned that fire is not just a tool, but a language.

When Rob and his wife Emily opened Helen’s doors in 2020 on Birmingham’s Second Avenue North, they set out to build more than a restaurant. They wanted to create a gathering place — a table where stories are told, traditions are honored, and every guest feels like family.

The Kitchen

Cooking with Fire

At the heart of Helen is a custom hardwood coal grill — the spiritual descendant of grandmother Helen’s cooking fire. Every protein that crosses our grill is kissed by live flame, building layers of flavor that simply cannot be replicated any other way.

Our approach is simple: start with the best ingredients we can find, prepare them with care and intention, and let the fire do what it does best. The result is food that feels both timeless and revelatory — deeply rooted in Southern tradition, yet unmistakably modern.
