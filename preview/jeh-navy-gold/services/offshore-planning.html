Overview

Global Jurisdictions,
Superior Protection

International asset protection planning leverages the unique advantages offered by foreign jurisdictions to create structures that are extraordinarily difficult for domestic creditors to penetrate. At JE Holdings, we design compliant offshore strategies that add powerful layers of protection beyond what domestic planning alone can achieve.

Our offshore planning encompasses international trusts in jurisdictions known for strong asset protection laws, foreign LLCs, international business companies (IBCs), and multi-jurisdictional holding structures. We work with established legal frameworks in the Cook Islands, Nevis, Belize, the Cayman Islands, and other premier jurisdictions known for debtor-friendly legislation.

Every offshore structure we create is designed with full compliance in mind. We ensure adherence to all U.S. reporting requirements, including FBAR, FATCA, and Form 3520 obligations, so your international planning is both powerful and legally sound.

Key Benefits

Why Offshore Planning
Delivers Superior Protection

International structures offer unique advantages that domestic planning alone cannot provide.

Enhanced Protection

Foreign jurisdictions add additional layers of protection that domestic creditors find extraordinarily difficult to penetrate. The requirement to litigate in a foreign court, under foreign law, with foreign legal counsel creates formidable barriers that deter even the most aggressive plaintiffs.

Global Diversification

Spread your assets across multiple jurisdictions to reduce concentration risk and increase protection depth. Diversifying internationally ensures that no single legal system or political environment can jeopardize your entire asset base.

Compliant Structures

Every offshore strategy we design is fully compliant with U.S. reporting obligations. We navigate the complex landscape of FBAR, FATCA, Form 3520, and other international reporting requirements to ensure your structures are both powerful and legally defensible.
