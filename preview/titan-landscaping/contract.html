04 — Terms
Agreement Terms

A summary of key terms is provided below. This agreement is subject to the complete Williams Digital Terms of Service, which are incorporated by reference.

1. Payment
A 50% deposit is due upon signing this agreement. The remaining 50% balance is due upon project completion, prior to the final site launch. Portal setup fees follow the same 50/50 structure. Monthly hosting and maintenance subscription payment links will be sent separately by Williams Digital upon service activation and are billed on the 1st of each month. Late payments beyond 14 days may result in service suspension.
2. Timeline
Williams Digital will deliver a staging preview within 7–14 business days of receiving all content and materials. Portal setup requires an additional 5–7 business days after website completion. Final launch occurs after client approval. Timelines may be extended if the Client delays in providing required materials, feedback, or approvals.
3. Revisions
This agreement includes up to two (2) rounds of revisions after the initial staging preview. Additional revision rounds may be subject to a supplemental fee agreed upon by both parties. A "revision round" is defined as a single consolidated set of changes submitted within 5 business days of receiving the staging preview.
4. Content & Materials
The Client is responsible for providing all written copy, images, logos, and media assets. Williams Digital may use professional stock imagery where client-provided assets are unavailable, at no additional cost. The Client warrants that all content provided is owned by the Client or properly licensed.
5. Ownership & Intellectual Property
Upon receipt of full and final payment, the Client receives ownership of all custom code, design assets, and content created specifically for the website. The portal platform remains the intellectual property of Williams Digital and is licensed to the Client for use under an active hosting plan. Williams Digital retains the right to showcase the completed work in its portfolio.
6. Portal Licensing
The Client & Staff Portal is provided as a hosted service under an active monthly subscription. The portal software, code, and design remain the property of Williams Digital. The Client receives a non-exclusive, non-transferable license to use the portal while the monthly hosting fee is current. Portal access may be suspended if monthly payments are more than 30 days past due.
7. Data & Privacy
All client and business data stored in the portal belongs to the Client. Williams Digital will not access, share, or sell client data except as necessary to provide the service. Upon cancellation, the Client may request an export of all portal data within 30 days. Data will be securely deleted after 60 days post-cancellation.
8. Hosting & Maintenance
Website design and development are covered under the one-time tier fee. Ongoing hosting, domain renewal, and website maintenance are the Client's responsibility unless a separate maintenance agreement is arranged at $125/month. Portal hosting is separate at $150/month and includes all services outlined in the portal add-on breakdown.
9. Limitation of Liability
Williams Digital's total liability under this agreement shall not exceed the total amount paid by the Client. Williams Digital shall not be liable for any indirect, incidental, consequential, or special damages, including but not limited to loss of revenue, profit, data, or business opportunities.
10. Cancellation
If the Client cancels after work has begun, the 50% deposit is non-refundable. Any completed work will be delivered. Monthly portal and maintenance subscriptions may be cancelled at any time with 30 days written notice. No refunds are provided for partial months.
11. Confidentiality
Both parties agree to keep project-related communications, credentials, and business information strictly confidential. Login credentials shared during the project will only be used for the stated purpose and will be securely discarded upon project completion.
12. Force Majeure
Neither party shall be liable for delays or failure to perform obligations under this agreement due to circumstances beyond their reasonable control, including but not limited to natural disasters, internet outages, government actions, pandemics, or other events of force majeure.
