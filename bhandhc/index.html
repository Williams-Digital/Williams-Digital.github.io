Our Story

Preserving Walker County's Heritage

The Bankhead House & Heritage Center showcases the past, present, and future of Walker County and the surrounding area through a wide variety of exhibits that change every three to four months, along with hosting cultural events.

“It has long been our belief that our town needed a proper place to display and recognize our rich heritage, as well as a place to host cultural events.”

— John T. Oliver, Jr., Founding Board Member

Five Distinct Spaces

Each room tells a unique chapter of Walker County's story

Congressional Library

Honoring 111 years of continuous U.S. Congressional service by seven representatives from Walker County towns.

The Gallery

Our primary exhibit space features rotating themes that change every three to four months.

Military Tribute

Recognizing Walker County service members with personal artifacts that rotate periodically.

Tallulah Bankhead Room

Personal and family artifacts from the legendary actress whose family gives the house its name.

Walker County Room

Historical items celebrating the region's rich heritage with exhibits that rotate throughout the year.

Events

Come Celebrate With Us

From outdoor concerts under the stars to holiday celebrations, our events bring the community together throughout the year.

Heritage Fun Day

Our annual outdoor celebration brings the whole community together with music, food, and fun for all ages.

Spring & Summer

Events Under the Stars

Grab your blanket or lawn chair and meet us in the Amphitheater! Each spring and summer season we host events in our outdoor venue with music, movies, and other entertainment.

Year-round

Exhibit Opening Receptions

We kick off each new exhibit with a community reception to celebrate the installation and bring people together around art and history.

December

Holiday Open House

Join us in December for carolers, entertainment, and refreshments while you enjoy our beautiful holiday decorations throughout the historic house.
